Challenges

As a student in the university, struggling to meetup wtih lectures and tutorials can be quite the hassle. The process of looking for a particular tutorial of interest based on individual institutionsâ€™ lecture sessions that may depict examples and explanations exclusively tackled during lecture is stressful and time-consuming without the promise of finding any. Eventually, even when a tutorial is being organized, it may be scheduled at an unsuitable time for a student. This problem however, can be solved using the available technology to make it more flexible for students to engage in tutorials and make them downloadable to their local storage devices.

Role

For this project, I played the role of Product Designer and Developer

Research Process

I took my first step into the reseach process by interviewing students around the campus about how they feel about tutorials.

I was mostly interested in the following areas:

  • How often students welcome the idea of tutorials (especially from peers)
  • How often students consume online tutorials and how enjoyable is it for them compared to physical tutorials
  • What possible flaws have they encountered while using other platforms utilized for online tutorials
  • How often do students tutor themselves and if they (Tutors) enjoyed the process of tutoring

Discovery

After carrying out my research on the importance and need of self administered tutorials i arrived at the following conclusions

  • 82% of students are always happy to receive tutorials either from peers or not
  • 62% of students who are interested in tutorials prefer online tutorial to physical tutorials
  • 20% of students who are interested in tutorials considered watching "video" tutorials as stressfull after a hard day or long lectures
  • Students felt the need to download tutorials directly to their local storage devices to avoid continous streaming thereby saving data expenses
  • Students interested in tutorials felt there was a need to easily contact and hold conversations with tutors via chat
  • Students who tutored also learn more as they teach

After compiling all my findings, I went back to my drawing board and thought of the best way to satisfying all users' needs of the system (Making sure everybody is happy... At least most).

Deeper Insight

Having itemized the users' needs of the system, it was now time for me to deliberate on design features for the system. I matched and cross reference new ideas with existing ones while trimming down or totally stripping off unnecessary features. I love to call this phase the sorting phase; I trimmed down my first ideas of the system to really fit the target users needs. At this stage, I removed the unnecessary features for the app making sure it focuses only the users needs; for example, I actually intended to include a live streaming functionality on the app but at this stage I finalized it wasn't a feasible design on the app (yet) as many students won't always be chanced to hold a live tutorial session due to other personal or faculty extra-curicullar activities (both tutors and tutee alike).

Designing

Having carried out my research and arrived at my end users needs, the design goals of the system became clearer. I now know I need a system that can help students upload tutorials at will in more than just one media and also enable the beneficiaries of this tutorial to be able to download these tutorials and hold a conversation with their tutors. I tried to design the system to satisfy the needs of all categories of potential users as discovered from my research






I started out with the UI design using Adobe XD and I tried to fit in the many functionalities of the app neatly into the users' view in a user friendly manner, I iterated between a lot of design options before finally finding the perfect fit for the project. I made sure the user has access to all the features especially the most crucial ones in less clicks by placing them within the users' reach.



Final Results

I was finally able to arrive at the desired system. I hosted the app on the local server of my department serving thousands of students within the campus from which I was able to collect feedback from. I got a reasonable amount of positive feedback and the app also displayed a great performance when evaluated with lighthouse.
